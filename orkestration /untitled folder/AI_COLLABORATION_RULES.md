## üìä **STORY WRITING STANDARDS**

### Length Requirements:
- **Full Stories:** 1,800-2,000 words (Marcus/Keisha level depth)
- **Short Stories:** 500-800 words (snapshot format)
- **Module 08 Examples:** 200-300 words (maintenance focus)

### Required Structure (Full Stories):
1. **The Narrative** (150-200 lines)
   - Character introduction with cultural context
   - Current reality and stuck state
   - Observation patterns and self-fulfilling prophecies
   - Pain points with concrete examples
   - Internal dialogue

2. **The Shift** (50-80 lines)
   - Catalyst moment
   - Pivotal question that reframes reality
   - Initial new observations
   - Emotional response
   - Decision/commitment point

3. **The Practice** (100-150 lines)
   - Week 1: Initial rituals with specific details
   - Week 2: Evidence building with examples
   - Week 3+: Momentum and integration
   - Timeline progression

4. **The Transformation** (100-150 lines)
   - What stayed the same (circumstances)
   - What actually changed (behaviors, relationships)
   - Specific examples with dialogue
   - Before/after measurements
   - Timeline markers (Month 1, 2-3, 6 months)

5. **The Conclusion** (30-50 lines)
   - Character's reflection quote
   - Clear lesson connecting to quantum principle
   - Final state vs starting point

### Cultural Authenticity:
- Research accurate cultural details
- Include culturally specific observation patterns
- Use authentic context (not stereotypes)
- Show how cultural background influences patterns
- 75% American cities, 25% international (when doing full series)

### Character Diversity Guidelines:
- **Geographic spread:** West Coast, Southwest, Midwest, East Coast, South
- **Ethnic diversity:** Asian-American, African-American, Hispanic/Latino, Middle Eastern-American, European-American
- **Age range:** 20s through 60s
- **Professions:** Mix of blue collar, white collar, creative, healthcare, education
- **Life situations:** Singles, couples, families, various challenges

### Keep Issues Current & Relatable (Loosely 2025 / Past 10 Years):
- **Economic reality:** Cost of living crisis, housing affordability, gig economy, AI job displacement fears
- **Mental health:** Post-pandemic anxiety, burnout culture, social media comparison, isolation despite connectivity
- **Social issues:** Political polarization, climate anxiety, generational divides, work-life balance impossibility
- **Technology:** AI integration anxiety, remote work challenges, algorithm-driven lives, screen addiction
- **Healthcare:** Medical debt, mental health access gaps, chronic stress normalization
- **Real struggles:** Student loans, caring for aging parents, single-parent challenges, career pivots in 40s/50s
- **Modern pressures:** Hustle culture, performative wellness, cancel culture anxiety, information overload

**Guideline:** Loosely keep stories within 2025 context, or at minimum within the past 10 years (2015-2025). Avoid dated references that feel disconnected from current reality.

**Why this matters:** People need to see recognizable struggles. Generic or pre-2015 problems feel disconnected. Recent/current issues = immediate relatability.

## üìù **WRITING STYLE STANDARDS**

### Voice & Tone:
- Address reader directly in second person (confident, encouraging coach)
- Pair authority with warmth: cite science plainly, then translate to everyday language
- Keep energy high but grounded‚Äîavoid hype, sarcasm, or clinical detachment
- Use active voice and empowering verbs ("You can‚Ä¶", "Here's how to‚Ä¶")

### Module Structure Order:
1. The Quantum Principle
2. Easiest Explanation
3. The Math
4. Practical Strategies and Guidance
5. Advanced Exercises
6. Journaling Prompts
7. Reflection Activities
8. Summary & Outcomes
9. Case Studies/Stories
10. Closing Thought or Foundational Truth

### Language Mechanics:
- Spell out numbers zero through nine (unless equations or time: "five minutes" vs. "10 minutes")
- Avoid ALL-CAPS emphasis; use **bold** or *italics*
- Keep sentences concise (‚â§25 words ideal) while preserving nuance
- Use parallel structure in bullet lists
- Replace jargon with plain-English or define immediately

### Science Integration:
- Introduce quantum concepts with short narrative or thought experiment
- Follow technical descriptions with "what this means for you" paragraph
- Include equations when relevant, explain components line by line
- Reference psychological/neuroscience evidence briefly (mechanism or researcher name)

### Story Pattern:
- **Situation** ‚Üí **Shift** ‚Üí **Practice** ‚Üí **Transformation** ‚Üí **Lesson**
- Highlight: unchanged external facts vs. changed internal experience
- Conclude with direct quote/reflection reinforcing module's core message

### Consistency Checks:
- Section headers use Title Case and parallel phrasing
- Transitional phrases appear verbatim ("Now let's get practical")
- Reader = "you" / Examples = named third parties
- Closing sections restate "Foundational Truth" in **bold**
